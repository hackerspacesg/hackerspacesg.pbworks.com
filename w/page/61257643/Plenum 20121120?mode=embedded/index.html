Event incident/policy

 

- what happened?

Member of Hackerspace, Andrew Wee, proposes an Internet related marketing event. However, this event was not approved by the events team. Justin Lee from the events team mentioned that he had received expressions of concern from Laurence and CJ regarding the nature of the event. Justin suggested that the issue be discussed at the plenum and Andrew tried to seek clarifications from Laurence and CJ.

 

Subsequently, Andrew decided that he did not want to defend his case at the plenum. He ended his membership in Nov.

 

What should have happened?

- General principle: members' use of the space is not subject to veto by other members.

- General principle: a wide range of events is desirable, close contact between people with very different backgrounds has been part of HSG's approach from the outset

- General principle: if we get to the point where we have so many event bookings that we're having to turn people away then we will have *succeeded*.

- General principle: there is a concern that members may join solely to have free use of space for events; this is not a problem, either:

-- it's the sort of technical event that everyone will turn up to anyway, then it will be worthwhile for that reason

-- it's the sort of event which isn't, which broadens contact with people with different backgrounds

 

- Member proposes an event (non-member may propose, but must have a sponsoring/hosting member)

- Events officer(s) consider:

-- legality

-- safety

-- resource constraints

- If there are concerns about any of these (and in particular about legality)

-- refer to proposer for clarification

-- if still a concern: refer to plenum (proposer to argue own case at plenum)

- If there are no concerns then the event is scheduled (published through calendar)

- If a member raises concerns after scheduling but before the event, the event(s) officers are free to reconsider and refer an event to plenum despite previous acceptance. The member(s) raising concerns are expected to be present at the next plenum to express their concerns. This should be treated as a last resort for dealing with unexpected differences of opinion as cancelling events does create ill-feeling towards HackerspaceSG

- Event officer(s) are asked to get to decisions - including referral to plenum - as early as possible as indecision also causes ill-feeling (and as in Andrew's case, member resignation)
